Bihari Sharma

Indian Tabla Artist • Contributor to Miles Davis Sessions

About

Bihari Sharma is an Indian-born tabla player known for his unique contributions to Western jazz fusion. He performed on landmark Miles Davis recording sessions, bringing classical Indian rhythm into contemporary jazz experimentation.

Notable Work & Discography

Big Fun (1974)
Contribution: Tabla / Indian Percussion
Bitches Brew Sessions (Recorded 1969–1970)
Contribution: Tabla (suggested by guitarist John McLaughlin)
Additional Miles Davis Studio Recordings
Contribution: Tambura & Indian rhythmic textures

Style & Influence

Sharma’s playing integrates traditional Indian rhythmic cycles (tala) with experimental fusion structures. His collaborations helped introduce Indian percussion to modern jazz audiences.
